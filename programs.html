Programs

Support that meets people where they are

ZCUS Alberta programs connect people to culture, guidance, mentorship, and trusted relationships across the community.

Newcomers

Arrival and settlement support

Welcoming conversations, community introductions, referrals, and practical navigation for Zimbabweans settling in Alberta.

  • Community orientation
  • Referral support
  • Family and peer connection

Seniors

Connection and care

Spaces for elders to stay connected, share wisdom, and remain visible in community life.

  • Social connection
  • Cultural continuity
  • Community check-ins

Youth

Leadership and identity

Youth programming supports confidence, mentorship, belonging, and pathways for contribution.

  • AIFY program pathway
  • Mentorship
  • Skills and leadership

Business owners

Networking and community visibility

Business owners and professionals can connect with peers, support events, and contribute expertise to community programs.

  • Networking
  • Community sponsorship
  • Mentorship opportunities

How programs are built

The society keeps programming practical and community-led, with room for partners, funders, and volunteers to strengthen delivery.

  • ListenGather community needs from members, families, youth, seniors, and volunteers.
  • CoordinateMatch people with programs, events, referrals, mentors, and partner resources.
  • SupportProvide welcoming spaces where people can learn, contribute, and stay connected.
